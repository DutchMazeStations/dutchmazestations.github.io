The Dutch were a major player in the spice trade in the 1600s. They established the Dutch East India company in what is present-day Indonesia. The purpose of this joint-stock company was to gain a trading monopoly of certain spices, such as nutmeg, cinnamon, mace and cloves. The Dutch violently conquered the islands in this region and forced the residents to only sell these products to them. The Dutch enslaved and killed many native populations and left many to starve.
